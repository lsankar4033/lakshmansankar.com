Hi! My name is Lakshman Sankar. It's a pleasure to meet you :).

Sometimes I'm a programmer. Other times I'm a growth hacker. Other times I'm a writer.

I believe that we're living in an age of critical transitions because of how quickly we're networking as a society. If we aren't deliberate in our response to these transitions, we're sure to be swept up into suboptimal outcomes.

Right now, I'm spending most of my time on blockchains and other peer-to-peer distributed trust networks. Cryptography shifts power from institutions with capital to the individual and I believe that these networks have the power to completely upend how we've organized ourselves in the past. I find it likely that many of the group activities we currently rely on an administrator for will be governed by blockchains in the future.

Some of my other interests are:

  1. Functional programming, writing, and math as exercises in clear thinking. I believe that all ideas can be cleanly expressed and that doing so can be practiced.
  2. Physical fitness. I share Socrates' sentiments. I love pushing my body to its boundaries and growing through those boundaries. I'm also just an endorphin junkie.
  3. AI and Machine Learning. Another important 'critical transition' is the outsourcing of most decision-making to machine intelligences. I've spent most of my career and education building and examining such intelligences.
